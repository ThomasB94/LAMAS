The Game

The Game is a card game that can be played with 1 to 5 players. It is a cooperative game where player play together against the game. Instead of a traditional deck, the game uses cards numbered from 2 to 99. The game is setup with four cards on the table; two 1s and two 100s. Each player is dealt a number of cards from the deck, depending on how many players play. The goal of the game is for every player to discard all of their cards. This can be done on either of the four cards on table by placing cards larger than the current cards on the stack on the 1 cards and cards lower than the current cards on the stack on the 100 cards. Every round each player must discard at least two cards, but there is no maximum. The caveat is that players can communicate, but with some restrictions. Players can't mention the cards they have, but they can give vague comments such as: "I have a good card for this stack" or "My cards won't be good for this stack".

The knowledge and the communication of players can be captured using epistemic and public announcement logic. In this project, we will model all the knowledge in the game and how public announcement can change this knowledge. This should give a good strategy to play the game optimally.

Though the game seems simple, playing with the aforementioned rules would lead to an explosion of knowledge states. Therefore, we will simplify the game in the following ways. We will play with only two players and two stacks of cards on the table, one that goes from 1 to 100 and one that goes from 100 to 1. We introduce two possible announcements that players can make, namely: "I have a good card for this stack" and "I have a bad card for this stack". The first announcement indicates that a player has a card that can be 1 to 5 off from the card on the stack, while the second announcement indicates that a player has a card that is more than 5 off from the current card.
